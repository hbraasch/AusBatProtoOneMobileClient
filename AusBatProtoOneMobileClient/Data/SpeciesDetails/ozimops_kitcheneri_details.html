South-western Free-tailed Bat
Ozimops kitcheneri
(McKenzie, Reardon and Adams, 2014)

Description This is a medium-sized species with a forearm length of 34.3 mm (32.7 to 35.4 mm) and weight of 8.7 g (7.5 to 10.5 g). The fur on the back is usually dark grey-brown with paler bases and a lighter belly. The bare skin of the face and wings is darkly pigmented. The ears are triangular and not joined across the top of the head. The fur colour tends to be grey-brown, dorsally and lighter ventrally, but variable in length. The skull is very flattened.

Roost Habits They usually roost in tree hollows but small colonies have been found under roofs in urban areas. Like other free-tailed bats, it can scurry about on the ground, which may explain why it has sometimes been caught in pitfall traps.

Habitat It forages over eucalypt forests and woodlands, arid and semiarid SW and Coolgardie woodlands of WA. They have been found in a variety of habitats from tall forests, open woodland, mallee and coastal heathlands.

Vocalisations and Echolocation calls 
Its echolocation calls are well suited to foraging in uncluttered habitat: in search mode, they are shallow frequency-modulated, low duty-cycle calls with most energy between 25.5 and 27.5kHz.
